# Interview Template

<!--
FACILITATION GUIDE — Service Designer (Research)
=================================================
Mode: Reference artifact (Diátaxis: Reference)

This is the generic interview template for the {{PRODUCT_NAME}} design package.
Copy this file, rename it with the next sequential number and a short identifier
(e.g. 02-interview-sofia.md), then fill in the sections for each participant.

At least ONE completed interview is required before the Design Foundation
Checkpoint is cleared. A self-interview (you interviewing yourself as primary
user) counts.

CONVERSATION FLOW:
1. Establish participant context (who, role, relationship to the problem)
2. Explore current reality (how they handle the problem domain today)
3. Surface tools and workarounds
4. Capture key quotes verbatim — these become evidence for design decisions
5. Extract pain points and risks
6. Synthesise takeaways

INTERVIEW QUESTIONS BY ROUND:

## Round 1: Participant Context
- "Who is this interview with? (Name, role, organisation type)"
- "How often do you encounter the problem this product addresses?"
- "What's your current process, end-to-end, for handling it?"
- "What tools or systems do you rely on today?"

## Round 2: Current Reality
- "Walk me through the last time you dealt with this. Start from how you found out."
- "How do you decide what to prioritise and what to skip?"
- "What does the full process look like from start to finish?"
- "How many people are involved? What roles do they play?"
- "How long does the whole thing take?"

## Round 3: Pain Points
- "What is the most frustrating part?"
- "Where do you waste the most time?"
- "Have you ever missed something you should have caught? What happened?"
- "Have you ever invested significant effort in something that turned out to be a
  bad fit? What made it look promising initially?"
- "What information do you wish you had earlier?"

## Round 4: Key Quotes
- Capture verbatim — exact words, not paraphrase.
- Look for: strong emotion, repetition, contradiction, surprise, metaphor.
- These quotes anchor design rationale. "Users said X" is weak. A direct quote is evidence.

## Round 5: Risks & Takeaways
- "What was the single most important thing you learned?"
- "What risks or unknowns did this surface?"
- "What assumptions were confirmed? Which were challenged?"

SYNTHESIS NOTES:
After the interview, write up the artifact before the next session.
Memory is lossy. Write it while it's fresh.
-->
